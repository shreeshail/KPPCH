Rules and Regulations

  1. All students are expected to wake up early in the morning, get refreshed and compulsorily attend Prayer @7:00AM in Morning & @7:30PM in evening. Dress code for the prayer is - white panche & white shirt. Attendance will be taken and then food will be served. After 9:00PM Gates Closed.
  2. Students who wish to use their personal laptop/desktop in hostel should inform the management in advance and get the approval from Secretary. However, home theaters, loud speakers are strictly not allowed as it can cause disturbances to other students and also to neighbor residents.
  3. KPPC Library is equipped with most recent books for all academic studies. Students are most welcome to utilize library books and daily news papers free of cost. Contact Library Admin (student) to get the books. Also, it is advised that news papers should be read only in library premises and should not be carried to your rooms. If any such incidents found, entire month news paper bill has to be borne by that student.
  4. Each student taking admission should buy their own lunch plate, water bottle/glass, plastic bucket, jug, towel, pillow, bed etc for their personal use.
  5. Common utilities such as water purifier, solar, bunker beds, study chairs should be used responsibly. Any damage to these devices/equipments has to be borne by the respective student who damages it.
  6. Food serving in the dining hall should be done by two students every day based on their name in the attendance register. If any student is absent/on leave, he should serve the food the next day when he reports back to hostel.
  7. Students are advised to keep their room clean all the time. Management /SSA/warden would visit randomly any time. Lunch carry bags or any other garbage should not be put in any floor. Which will creates bad smell around, and students will get disturbance on their studies.
  8. Weekly once (on Sunday) each floor should be cleaned by the students staying in the respective floors. Floor in-charge (student) will assign this to all students in round-robin fashion including him.
  9. Bathrooms and toilets should be maintained clean. Do not throw shampoo, soap, toothpaste covers in the bath rooms.
  10. For easy administration of the day to day maintenance, students will be assigned few responsibilities by the warden. You need to obey it and carry out your responsibility carefully.
  11. Students are advised to have good interaction with Senior Students Association (SSA) which is carrying out lot of initiatives/activities for the development of hostel students and infrastructure. SSA and hostel management closely monitors educational performance of each student regularly. As a STUDENT, STUDY is your first preference.
  12. Smoking, drinking and ragging is STRICTLY prohibited. If any student involves in any of these activities and gets caught, they will be sent-out of the hostel immediately without any notice and excuses.
  13. For any other questions/clarifications/help/guidance, students can contact hostel management or Senior Students Association.
  14. Minimum 65% required in the previous year results, to get Admission for Next Academic Year.
  15. Unnecessary mistakes & shouting is Strictly Prohibited.
  16. Students should not absent/leave without intimation to management.
All students should obey and follow above rules strictly. If anybody violates, other students can intimate this to warden/management, and Strict/appropriate action will be taken against student violate the rules. ALL THE BEST TO ALL STUDENTS..!!!

History

Diwan Sir K P Puttanna Chetty

Diwan Bahadur Sir Krishnarajapur Palligonde Puttanna Chetty CIE (1856–1938) was an administrator, bureaucrat and philanthropist who served as the first President of the Bangalore municipality.

Early life and education

Puttanna Chetty was born in 1856 in a Lingayat family of Bangalore. He was educated at the Central College, Bangalore and entered the Mysore civil service in 1875.

Career

Chetty served as a traffic manager in the Mysore state railways from 1884 to 1898 and as Deputy Commissioner from 1898 to 1906. From 1906 to 1912, he also served as a member of the Bangalore City Council. In 1913, Puttanna Chetty was elected President of the Bangalore municipality and he served till 1919, inaugurating the Victoria Hospital and making many public donations during his tenure. Puttanna Chetty was elected to the Mysore Legislative Council in 1925. Chetty served in various appointments during his life – as Chairman of the Bank of Mysore and Central Co-Operative Bank, Bangalore, Chairman of the Sri Krishnarajendra Mills and fellow of the Mysore University. Puttanna Chetty served as President at Rao Bahadhur Dharmapravartha Gubbi Thotadappa Charities from 1910 to 1938.


Bangalore Town Hall

Bangalore Town Hall is a neoclassical municipal building in Bangalore, India. The town hall is named after the philanthropist and former president of Bangalore city municipality, Sir K.P Puttanna Chetty. The foundation stone for the building was laid by the then Maharaja of Mysore, Krishna Rajendra Wodeyar on March 6, 1933. The building was completed on September 11, 1935. It was inaugurated by crown prince Kanteerava Narasimharaja Wadiyar. Built by Sir Mirza Ismail, the building features a flight of steps leading to the entrance porch resting on Tuscan columns with identical columns extending on either sides. Financial assistance was provided by Kanteerava Wodeyar. Due to improper acoustics, a renovation was proposed estimated at Rs 1,000,000 in 1976. Postponements delayed renovations till March 1990, when the building was finally closed for renovation. The cost then was 6.5 million rupees (approximately US$371,400). The auditorium has two floors with a previous total capacity of 1,038 seats. The seating capacity has been reduced to 810 following the renovations.

Death

Puttanna Chetty died in 1938 at the age of eighty two.

Honours

Puttanna Chetty was made a Diwan Bahadur in 1911. In 1917, he was made a Companion of the Order of the Indian Empire and was subsequently made a Knight Bachelor in 1925. He was also awarded the 1911 Durbar Medal and the Gold Kaiser-i-Hind Medal in 1914.
